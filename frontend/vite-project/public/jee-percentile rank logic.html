NTA Rank Calculation from Percentile (JEE Main 2026)

1. Why Normalization is Required

JEE (Main) is conducted in multiple shifts. Each shift may have different question papers, and difficulty levels may vary slightly. To ensure fairness, NTA uses a Normalization Procedure based on Percentile Score.

2. Definition of Percentile Score

Percentile Score represents the percentage of candidates in a particular shift who scored equal to or less than a given candidate.

Formula:

Percentile Score (TP1) = 
100 × (Number of candidates in the shift with raw score ≤ candidate's raw score)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Total number of candidates who appeared in that shift
  • Calculated shift-wise.
  • Range: 0 to 100.
  • Calculated up to 7 decimal places.
  • Highest raw score in each shift = 100 percentile.
  • Percentile ≠ Percentage of marks.
  • Total percentile is NOT an average of subject percentiles.

3. Step-by-Step Normalization Process

Step 1: Distribution of Candidates

Candidates are randomly distributed across shifts to ensure no bias.

Step 2: Calculation of Shift-wise Percentile

  • Raw scores are computed.
  • Total Raw Score percentile (TP1) is calculated per shift.
  • Highest raw score → 100 percentile.
  • Lowest raw score percentile depends on total candidates in that shift.

Step 3: Compilation of NTA Score

Percentile scores of Total Raw Score from all shifts are merged. These merged percentile scores are called NTA Scores.

NTA Score replaces raw marks and is used for:

  • Merit List preparation
  • All India Rank (AIR)
  • Category Rank

4. Multi-Session Handling (Session 1 & Session 2)

  • NTA Score calculated separately for Session 1 and Session 2.
  • If candidate appears in both sessions:
  • Best of the two Total NTA Scores is used for ranking.
  • Subject-wise best is NOT considered — only Total NTA Score.

5. Preparation of Rank

5.1 All India Rank (AIR)

  • Prepared after completion of Session 2.
  • Based on merged best Total NTA Scores.
  • Higher NTA Score → Better Rank.

5.2 Category Rank

  • Prepared separately for GEN, OBC-NCL, SC, ST, EWS, PwBD.
  • Based on respective category NTA Scores.

6. Tie-Breaking Rules (Paper 1 - B.E./B.Tech)

  1. Higher NTA Score in Mathematics
  2. Higher NTA Score in Physics
  3. Higher NTA Score in Chemistry
  4. Lower proportion of incorrect to correct answers (overall)
  5. Lower proportion in Mathematics
  6. Lower proportion in Physics
  7. Lower proportion in Chemistry
  8. If still tied → Same Rank assigned

7. Cut-off Determination Across Shifts

If eligibility percentile differs across shifts, the lowest percentile corresponding to eligibility is used as cut-off for all shifts.

Example:

  • Shift 1 → 40% marks = 78 percentile
  • Shift 2 → 40% marks = 79 percentile
  • Final cut-off = 78 percentile (applied to both shifts)

8. Final Result Compilation Flow

Raw Marks (Shift-wise)
        ↓
Shift-wise Percentile Calculation
        ↓
Merged Percentile Scores = NTA Score
        ↓
Best of Two Sessions (if applicable)
        ↓
All India Rank (AIR)
        ↓
Category Rank

9. Important Notes

  • Rank Card available online only.
  • No re-evaluation or re-checking allowed.
  • Only AIR used for JoSAA/CSAB admissions.
  • NTA is examination body, not admission authority.
